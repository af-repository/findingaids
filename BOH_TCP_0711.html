Historical note

The Five City Tobacco Control Collaborative (also called the Five City Tobacco Control Program Initiative) is a state-funded partnership between the Health Departments of Cambridge, Chelsea, Everett, Revere, and Somerville that promotes and enforces tobacco control policies, educates retailers on policy and regulations, and educates the public on the health hazards of tobacco. The Somerville Health Department partnered with the cities of Everett and Revere in 1994, with the cities of Chelsea and Cambridge joining in 1995 and 1997 respectively. The program focuses on policy development and enforcement regarding Youth Access to tobacco products and Environmental Tobacco Smoke. The program is funded by the Massachusetts Department of Public Health.

ABT Associates, Inc. Records, 1993-1996 

Scope and Contents

Contains instructional material pertaining to ABT Associates’ independent evaluation of the Massachusetts Tobacco Control Program. Records include a 1994 evaluation report, correspondence between ABT Associates and the Somerville Board of Health, and data submission guidelines for the Board of Health.
