Historical note

Somerville's preparations for civilian defense action began in September 1940 when the city established a local branch of the Massachusetts Committe on Public Safety. In response to the United States entering World War II, the city established the Somerville Council for Municipal Defense on December 11, 1941 “for the preservation of health and the protection of persons and property”. The Council’s five members were appointed by the Mayor and approved by the Aldermen. The Council, also called the Somerville Municipal Defense Committee, organized men and women volunteers in various civilian defense roles including positions in the Administrative Staff, Report Center Staff, Protection Division including the Auxiliary Police and Fire Departments, Medical Division, Services and Supplies Division, Planning and Technical Division, Health and Social Services Division, Evacuation Division, Public Information Division, Women’s Division, and Hospital Division. Women also volunteered in the Somerville unit of the Massachusetts Women's Defense Corps (MWDC). Civilian Defense was merged into Emergency Management after the 1950s.

Sources:

Doherty, Richard P. History of the Massachusetts Committee on Public Safety 1940-1945. Boston, 1945.

Somerville Board of Aldermen Meeting Minutes. 1941.

Personnel Records, 1942-1943 

Scope and Contents

Contains personnel records of volunteers and staff who participated in Somerville Civilian Defense activities organized by the Somerville Council for Municipal Defense. Personnel records include Registration Cards, Personal History Reports, Index Cards, and Roll Lists. The Registration Cards and Personal History Reports include information such as the volunteer's civil defense division or position, name, address, date of birth, nationality, physical description, family information, occupation, education, organization affiliations, military and public service, and firearm possession. The Index Cards and Roll Lists include the volunteer's civil defense division or position, name, address, and registration dates.
