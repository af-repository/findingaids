Historical Note

The Somerville Redevelopment Authority (SRA) was established in 1956 to implement the Cityâ€™s urban renewal plans. In 1965 the SRA began work on its first project, the Inner Belt Urban Renewal Project (Project No. Mass. R-112), in response to the Stateâ€™s proposed plans for the Inner Belt Expressway (Interstate 695). The Inner Belt Urban Renewal Project was initially a 20-year plan aimed at revitalizing industry in East Somerville by redeveloping the Inner Belt area bounded by Washington Street to the north, the Somerville-Boston (Charlestown) boundary to the east, what is now New Washington Street to the south, and the Lowell Commuter Rail line to the west. After plans for the Inner Belt Expressway fell through, the SRA modified Project Mass. R-112 (retitled Cobble Hill Urban Renewal Project) in 1974 to change industrial zoning in the Cobble Hill area to business zoning. The Project resulted in the construction of Cobble Hill Road, New Washington Street, and new business, retail, and residential development.

Scope and Contents

Contains Somerville Redevelopment Authority (SRA) records pertaining to Project No. Mass. R-112 (also called the Inner Belt Urban Renewal Project and, from 1974, the Cobble Hill Urban Renewal Project) from 1965 to the late 1970s. Administration Records also include SRA meeting minutes (1989-2002) and miscellaneous correspondence pertaining to general SRA business.
