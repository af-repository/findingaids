Somerville Commission for Persons with Disabilities, 1987-2003 

Historical note

The Somerville Commission for Persons with Disabilities was established by Ordinance in 1992 with a mission to promote an environment free of discrimination against persons with disabilities and to ensure City compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and anti-discrimination regulations. The Commission existed previously as the Somerville Commission for the Handicapped (circa 1989). The Commission works closely with the Executive Office on Disability and Compliance and was organized for a time under the Somerville Human Rights Commission.

Somerville Commission for Women, 1988-2014 

Historical note

The Somerville Commission for Women aims to ensure the equality of all women through advocacy, education, and outreach on issues such as anti-violence, financial literacy, empowerment, coalition building, and monitoring and supporting legislation. The Commission, created in March 1988, is made up of an appointed Executive Director, appointed volunteer Commissioners who serve three-year terms, volunteers who serve on committees and help organize events and activities, and college interns who support the Executive Director and Commissioners and help plan and implement Commission projects.

In addition to organizational committees such as the Steering, Outreach, Fundraising, Public Relations, and Newsletter Committees, the Commission has organized a number of permanent and ad-hoc social committees to address issues such as equality in the judicial process, diversity, domestic violence, empowerment, financial literacy, physical and mental health, pay equity, and violence against women. The Commission also collaborates with local women's service agencies and participates in state and national women's initiatives.

The Executive Directors of the Commission are as follows: Rose Boardman (1988-1992), Maria C. Altamore (1992-1994) Maya Hasegawa (1994-1999), Christie Mase (1999-2004), Sonja Darai (2004- 2009). Many of the records in this subseries were compiled by the Executive Directors.

Scope and Contents

Contains Commission administration records, financial records, meeting minutes, records of Commission programming and events, records of collaborations with local, state, and national social service groups, and informational resources relevant to Commission activities.

Financial Literacy Project, 2005-2008 

Historical note

The SCW initiated a Financial Literacy Project in 2005 based on the Womankind Financial Literacy Project to provide women with resources on money management. The Commission formed a Financial Literacy Taskforce in May 2008 to support services that empower and educate people about money, risky financial ventures, simple budgeting and planning, and local resources for finance management. The Taskforce is made up of Somerville professionals working on financial literacy, poverty prevention, and money services. Tufts Scholar interns worked with the Executive Director to create a report of financial literacy programs in Somerville, to plan and execute the Financial Literacy Fair, to create material for public awareness, and to bring social agencies together to collaborate on financial literacy in Somerville.

Somerville Human Rights Commission, 1974-2013 

Historical note

The Somerville Human Rights Commission was established in 1993 to uphold the City’s Human Rights Ordinance passed the same year. The Commission works with the City and community organizations to protect the constitutional, civil, and human rights of all people in Somerville and promote tolerance and mutual respect by providing education about human rights issues, investigating cases of unlawful discrimination, and ensuring municipal compliance with human rights regulations. The Ordinance grants the Commission full enforcement powers such as summons, administration of oath, and review of documents.

The Commission consists of an appointed Executive Director and appointed Commissioners and volunteers. The Commission initially oversaw the Commission for Persons with Disabilities. Commission Executive Directors are as follows: Yves-Rose SaintDic (1993-1994), Jamila Xible (1994-1996), Noreen Burke (1998-2002), Shenandoah Titus (2002-2006), Mary Lu Mendonca (2006-2007).
