Historical note

The function of the Board of Health is to promote public health in Somerville. The Board decides upon and enforces health regulations and policies for the city. Issues addressed by the Board include tobacco control, emergency preparedness, disease control and prevention, waste disposal, and pest control.

The Board consists of members appointed by the Mayor and subject to confirmation by the Board of Aldermen. As per Section 38 of the City Charter, members of the Board of Heath serve "two for one year, and one for two years, and each year thereafter one to serve for the term of one year, and one for the term of two years. Vacancies occurring in the board may be filled in the same manner, but only for the unexpired term." A physician must always fill one seat on the Board.

Laurence Howard, Executive Clerk Records, circa 1900-1939  --  5.0 Cubic feet

Scope and Contents

This series contains the papers of the Board of Health Executive Clerk, Laurence S. Howard. His correspondence reflects the business of the Board of Health, as well as the major public health movements and concerns in the beginning of the 20th century. Records include documents relating to administrative matters and the Board’s actions to promote health and prevent the spread of diseases. This series also broadly documents the development of vaccinations and changes in the milk and dairy industry. Also included are Howard’s personal papers that reflect his love of local theater and participation in fraternal orders.
